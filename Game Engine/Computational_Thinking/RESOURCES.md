# Learning Resources for Problem Solving

Here is a list of free and highly-recommended resources to master logical thinking and computational problem solving.

## Must-Read Books (Free/Open Source)
1. **[Think Like a Programmer](https://nostarch.com/thinklikeaprogrammer)** by V. Anton Spraul: Excellent for learning how to approach problems.
2. **[Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs (SICP)](https://mitpress.mit.edu/sites/default/files/sicp/full-text/book/book.html)**: The "Wizard Book". Very advanced, but the foundation of CS.
3. **[Introduction to Algorithms (CLRS)](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262046305/introduction-to-algorithms/)**: The bible of algorithms.

## Websites for Practice
1. **[Exercism](https://exercism.org/)**: Mentored coding tracks. Great for learning the "right" way to solve problems.
2. **[Project Euler](https://projecteuler.net/)**: Mathematical and logical puzzles that require programming to solve.
3. **[LeetCode / HackerRank](https://leetcode.com/)**: Industry-standard for technical interview problems.
4. **[Brilliant.org](https://brilliant.org/)**: (Paid, but has great free daily challenges) Focuses on visual logic and math.

## YouTube Channels
1. **[Computerphile](https://www.youtube.com/user/Computerphile)**: Deep dives into how computers and algorithms work.
2. **[The Primeagen](https://www.youtube.com/@ThePrimeTimeagen)**: Practical advice on problem-solving and software engineering.
3. **[Tom Scott - The Basics](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL96C35uN7xGI9HGfUC0AF7A_C6_95m0S6)**: Understanding the "Why" behind technology.

## Logical Thinking Exercises
- **Sudoku / Chess**: Excellent for training your brain to see several steps ahead.
- **Rubber Duck Debugging**: Explaining your problem out loud.
- **Flowcharting**: Drawing your logic before you type it.
